Introduction

What Do Employers Want?

That’s the most basic question of any career search. This exercise is designed to help you answer that question by “sampling the field” of your choice and identifying the most commonly sought skills, experiences, credentials and characteristics.

Technology

How to Beat the Applicant Tracking System

Applicant tracking systems (ATS) are software that manage the hiring process. They provide an interface for recruiters to view and search resumes.

"The ATS matches resumes with open positions by searching for keywords from the job description."

Strategy

Advantages for Job Seekers

By loading your resume with job-related keywords, you take advantage of automated screening tools that index and crawl submissions.

This brings your resume to the forefront, where it might otherwise have been overlooked through manual review.

Pitfalls

The Disadvantages

If you apply to every position with the same resume, the ATS will be your downfall.

  • ✕ Will not accept skills that are "close" or "almost."
  • ✕ Missing specific keywords leads to immediate denial.
Action Plan

Position Yourself for Success

Tailor & Focus

Emphasize relevant experience in areas critical to the specific position.

Keyword Integration

Use terms and phrases referenced in the job posting to ensure indexing.

Formatting Rules

Avoid ornate fonts, columns, or graphics. Use bold/caps in plain black text.

Network First

Optimizing is half the battle; networking is the ultimate step to finding opportunity.
