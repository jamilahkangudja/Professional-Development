Mail Merge: Word to Email

Personalize hundreds of emails using Excel and Microsoft Word.

Step 1: The Excel Foundation

Success in Mail Merge depends 90% on how you format your Excel file. If your headers are messy, Word won't know where to put your data.

Crucial Formatting Rules:

  • Row 1 Must Be Headers: Use simple, one-word headers (e.g., FirstName, LastName, EmailAddress).
  • No Blank Rows: Do not leave empty rows at the top or in the middle of your list.
  • Close the File: Word cannot "read" the Excel file if it is currently open in Excel.

Step 2: Connecting Word to Data

1 Open a blank Microsoft Word document and write your email draft.

2 Go to the Mailings tab at the top.

3 Click Select Recipients > Use an Existing List...

4 Find your saved Excel file. When prompted, select Sheet1$ and make sure the box for "First row contains headers" is checked.

Step 3: Inserting Merge Fields

Now you replace generic names with "Placeholders" (Merge Fields).

  1. Highlight the text you want to personalize (e.g., "Customer").
  2. In the Mailings tab, click Insert Merge Field.
  3. Select the header name that matches your Excel column (e.g., FirstName).
Pro Tip: Mail merge fields look like this: «FirstName». Make sure there is a space before and after the brackets, otherwise your text will look like "HiAlex" instead of "Hi Alex".

Step 4: Sending the Emails

  1. Click Preview Results in the Mailings tab to double-check the names.
  2. Click Finish & Merge > Send Email Messages.
  3. In the popup box:
    • To: Select the EmailAddress column.
    • Subject line: Type the subject your recipients will see.
    • Mail format: Choose HTML (recommended).
  4. Click OK. Outlook will now begin sending the emails!

Required Software: Microsoft Word, Microsoft Excel, and Microsoft Outlook.
