Teaching, Instructional Design, & Program Practice

This portfolio documents my approach to course design, program development, and student-centered learning in higher education. The work presented here reflects an emphasis on modular structure, reflective practice, and the intentional use of emerging tools to support learning outcomes.

About My Practice

My work sits at the intersection of teaching, instructional design, and program development. I design learning experiences for students who are navigating higher education with varied levels of academic preparation, professional exposure, and institutional familiarity—many of whom are first-generation college students.

Across my work, I prioritize clarity of structure, intentional scaffolding, and reflection as a tool for meaning-making. I approach design as an iterative process, refining content and learning pathways based on student engagement, feedback, and observed needs.

Pedagogical & Design Orientation

My design practice is grounded in clarity, structure, and accessibility for students with varied levels of academic and professional preparedness. I view instructional design as an iterative process—one that balances intentional scaffolding with opportunities for exploration, reflection, and growth.

  • Modular, Canvas-native course design
  • Scaffolded learning experiences that reduce cognitive overload
  • Reflection as a tool for meaning-making and skill articulation
  • Ethical, transparent use of AI to support—not replace—instruction
