Student Journey Map: Scott et al. Framework

A systematic analysis of student engagement, institutional friction, and restorative opportunities during high-stakes organizational transitions.

Dimensions Phase 1: Virtual Onboarding Phase 2: Hybrid Transition Phase 3: Crisis & Friction Phase 4: Restorative Pivot
Student Actions & Behaviors Participating in high-frequency Zoom meetings; building relationships digitally; managed self-expression. Attempting physical implementation of digital training; navigating "travel tax" for in-person requirements. Withdrawal from communication; missed deadlines; struggling with legacy expectations vs physical capacity. Engaging in coaching-based accountability; co-creating sustainable norms; prioritizing well-being.
Emotional State / "Pulse"
Optimistic / Performative

Sense of belonging is theoretical; safety in the digital screen.
Overwhelmed / Clashing

Physical exhaustion; anxiety over legacy standards.
Grief / Isolation

"Sustainability Trap"; belief that failure is inevitable and personal.
Relieved / Realigned

De-coupling identity from the role; psychological safety returning.
Institutional Touchpoints LMS, Virtual Town Halls, Group Chat Oversight. Physical Classrooms, Administrative Offices, Ritual Spaces. Compliance Notices, Conduct Hearings, Delinquency Emails. One-on-One Coaching, Resiliency Meetings, Policy Adjustments.
Pain Points & Barriers
  • Lack of physical bonding
  • Digital fatigue
  • The "Geographic Tax"
  • Ritual disconnect
  • Grief-induced silence
  • Pregnancy/Health barriers
  • Legacy pressure
  • Policy rigidity
  • Board misalignment
Strategic Opportunities Early identification of commuter students for tailored support. Implementing "Muscle Memory" workshops to bridge digital-to-physical gaps.
