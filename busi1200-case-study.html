BUSI 1200: Careers & Professional Development

Instructional Design Case Study

Course Snapshot

BUSI 1200 is an 8-week, first-year course focused on career readiness, reflection, and professional skill articulation. The course is designed for students with varying levels of academic and professional preparedness, including many first-generation college students.

Instructional Design Approach

Course content is delivered through modular Canvas-based pages that prioritize clarity, structure, and student engagement. Learning experiences are scaffolded to reduce overwhelm and emphasize practice-based learning.

  • Weekly modular structure
  • Clear prerequisites to unlock content
  • Practice → reflection → feedback loop
  • Low-stakes, high-engagement activities

Use of AI in Content Development

AI tools were used to support instructional design by prototyping content, generating examples, and refining language for clarity and accessibility. All AI-assisted outputs were reviewed, edited, and aligned to course learning objectives by the instructor.

Reflection & Iteration

Course design decisions were refined over time based on student engagement, feedback, and observed areas of confusion. Emphasis on modular structure and guided reflection helped students better articulate skills and career interests. Future iterations will continue to balance structure with flexibility.
