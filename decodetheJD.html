Decoding the JD.

Before you write, you must audit. Recruiters hide the "answers" to the test inside the job posting.

Step 1: Identify "Pain Points"

Companies don't hire people just because they have a degree; they hire to solve a problem. Scan the JD for recurring keywords. These are their "Pain Points."

Step 2: The Skill Extraction

Per UNT Standards, you need to identify two major skills from the requirements section. These will become the foundation of your Marketing Paragraph.

Case Study: Decoding a Real Role

Role Title

Junior Business Analyst (Entry Level)

Job Description

We are seeking a detail-oriented analyst to join our operations team. You will be responsible for interpreting complex data sets to provide actionable insights. The ideal candidate collaborates effectively with cross-functional teams and can present findings to stakeholders.

Key Requirements

  • • Ability to utilize Excel for budgetary analysis and performance reviews.
  • • Strong interpersonal skills with a focus on client satisfaction.
Skill Option 1: Technical

"Skill 1: Data Interpretation & Excel Analysis." Evidence needed: A project where you used Excel to solve a problem.

Skill Option 2: Soft Skill

"Skill 2: Collaborative Communication." Evidence needed: A time you worked in a group to meet a stakeholder's needs.

Audit Check: Is it on your Resume?

The skills you choose for your cover letter must already exist on your resume. You are simply expanding on the story behind those bullets.
