Plan, direct, or coordinate marketing policies and programs, such as determining the demand for products and services offered by a firm and its competitors, and identify potential customers.
Tasks Core Tasks

Formulate, direct, or coordinate marketing activities or policies to promote products or services, working with advertising or promotion managers.

Resume Strategy: Use "Formulate" to describe a time you created a plan for a club, internship, or class project. Example: "Formulated a digital outreach policy that expanded student engagement by 20%."

Identify, develop, or evaluate marketing strategy, based on knowledge of establishment objectives, market characteristics, and cost and markup factors.

Interview Tip: Be ready to explain how you identify opportunities. Mention using tools like SWOT analysis or competitor research to "evaluate strategy."

Direct the hiring, training, or performance evaluations of marketing or sales staff and oversee their daily activities.

Leadership Gap: Students often lack formal management experience. Close this gap by highlighting times you led a committee, mentored a peer, or managed a volunteer team.

Evaluate the financial aspects of product development, such as budgets, expenditures, research and development appropriations, or return-on-investment and profit-loss projections.

The "Math" Gap: Marketing isn't just creative. Employers want to see "ROI" or "Budget Management." Even if it was a $200 budget for a bake sale, track the ROI to prove you have a "financial mindset."

Consult with product development personnel on product specifications such as design, color, or packaging.

Soft Skill: This is about "Cross-functional Collaboration." On a resume, highlight a time you worked with someone outside your major (e.g., an engineer or artist) to build something.
