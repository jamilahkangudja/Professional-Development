The "Show, Don't Tell" Upgrade.

Compare these two versions of the same Marketing Paragraph. One makes claims; the other provides evidence.

Version 1: The "Teller"

"I am a hard worker and I have used Excel a lot in my classes at UNT. I am very motivated to use my skills to help American Airlines. I am also a team player who communicates well with others in group projects."

✗ Cliché Overload: Uses "Hard worker" and "Team player" which are subjective and overused.
✗ Zero Evidence: Says they used Excel "a lot" but doesn't say what they actually built or solved.
Version 2: The "Show-er"

"In my Audit and Assurance course at UNT, I utilized Excel to perform a budgetary analysis on a dataset of over 500 entries, identifying three significant financial discrepancies. This required cross-functional collaboration with a team of four to present our findings to the department head."

✓ Concrete Evidence: Replaced "used Excel a lot" with a specific dataset size and a measurable task.
✓ SOAR Method: Describes the Situation (Audit course), Action (Budgetary analysis), and Result (identified discrepancies).

The Key Transformation:

1.

Delete Adjectives

Remove words like "motivated" or "passionate."

2.

Add Numbers

Mention data sizes, team counts, or grade results.

3.

Bridge to JD

Use the exact phrasing from the Job Description requirements.
