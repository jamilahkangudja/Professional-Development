Module 3: Professional Persuasion

The Cover Letter Deep Dive.

If the resume is your factual history, the cover letter is your narrative. It’s the "Why" behind the "What," designed to connect the dots between your past and their future.

1

Bridge the Gap

It explains how disparate experiences on your resume unite to make you the perfect candidate for this specific role.

2

Show Soft Skills

A resume lists skills; a cover letter demonstrates them through storytelling and professional tone.

3

Answer Questions

Addressing gaps, relocation, or career pivots happens here, ensuring the recruiter doesn't have to guess.

A Visual Anatomy

YOUR CONTACT INFO
Paragraph 1: The Hook "This is what I want to do & Why I want to do it with you." Mention the role and your connection to the company.
Paragraph 2: The Marketing "This is what I can do for you." Match your degree and skills to the JD using specific evidence.
Paragraph 3: The Call to Action "I want an interview." State your availability, contact method, and thank them for their time.

Key Standards

Length

Strictly 1 page. 3 concise paragraphs are ideal.

Tone

Professional but personal. Limit "I" statements.

Evidence

Every claim needs a supporting example from your past.

Hover over sections to reveal their strategic purpose.

B The Quality Checklist

✔

Avoid Clichés

Remove "Team player" or "Highly motivated" unless backed by a specific project result.

✔

Name the Source

Always mention where you saw the job (e.g., Handshake, Company Website).

✔

Simple Formatting

Use standard fonts (10-12pt) and avoid graphics that confuse ATS scanners.

✔

No "I" Overload

Shift the focus to the employer. Instead of "I did X," try "X was a key achievement that..."
